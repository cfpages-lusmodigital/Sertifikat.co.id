# Topical Authority — sertifikat.co.id

## Role and boundary

`Sertifikat.co.id` is evidenced as a commercial information, referral, training, and certification-facilitation property. It publishes hundreds of occupational scheme pages, training categories, named LSP profiles, KADIN-related pages, consultation services, and calls to register. Repository copy sometimes describes the site itself as a “lembaga pelatihan dan sertifikasi,” uses “resmi BNSP,” and speaks as if “LSP kami” issues certificates. Local files do not by themselves prove that `Sertifikat.co.id` is an LSP, an accredited/licensed certification body, KADIN, BNSP, a regulator, or an authorized representative.

Until current primary records establish each relationship, the safe role is **independent facilitator/information and training marketplace that may connect participants with separately identified training providers, LSPs, assessors, and authorities**. Every commercial page must name the actual legal provider, certificate issuer, scheme owner, current license/accreditation status and scope, assessment location, fees, privacy controller, complaint route, and contractual counterparty. Training completion never guarantees competence, eligibility, assessment success, licensing, employment, promotion, or certificate recognition.

## Evidence audited

- Canonical repository: `https://github.com/cfpages-lusmodigital/Sertifikat.co.id.git`, branch `main`, commit `9b38a884bff8246bbb48374d399f417f24e6ec9e`.
- 2,406 tracked files and 507 HTML routes in `sitemap-complete.xml`/README.
- Route inventory: 403 `materi/*`, 56 `sertifikasi/*`, 17 `pelatihan/*`, plus program, LSP, KADIN, consultation, K3, author/category/archive, contact, portfolio, and company pages.
- `materi/*` spans construction, K3, mining, welding, machinery, administration, HR, IT, tourism, beauty, food safety, halal, finance, logistics, agriculture, care work, and many other occupations.
- `lembaga-sertifikasi` lists many named LSPs and claims BNSP licensing/national recognition; `kadin-institute` states cooperation with BNSP-licensed LSPs. These are commercial/site assertions, not verified primary evidence.
- Structured data names `Sertifikat.co.id` as an Organization/Person and uses the alternate name “Pelatihan & Sertifikasi Kompetensi Resmi BNSP di Berbagai Bidang.” That wording creates material authority-confusion risk.
- Site pages are largely dated April–May 2025. License, scope, scheme, regulation, partner, assessor, venue, fee, and recognition status are volatile and require current verification.

## Related-domain opportunities

Other owned education, safety, construction, software, and industry properties may independently cover subject skills. `Sertifikat.co.id` should link only when contextually useful and must never imply those properties grant legal authority, accreditation, or recognition.

## Consolidation plan

1. Inventory all 403 `materi` pages by current scheme, sector, issuer, intent, and content similarity. Keep verified useful URLs; merge stale/synonymous templates into SER-07 hubs.
2. Audit every named LSP/KADIN/partner page against current primary registries and written authorization. Correct, neutralize, noindex, or remove unsupported affiliation wording.
3. Separate training sales, assessment facilitation, certificate issuance, and consulting into distinct contracts/routes.
4. Add “last verified,” issuer, scheme code/version, license scope/status, source links, and responsible reviewer to every scheme/provider page.
5. No geographic clones; local pages require genuinely different authority, venue, schedule, or verified case substance.

## Internal-link architecture

SER-01 routes readers to scheme choice, provider verification, training, assessment, and credential checks. SER-02 links to relevant SER-07 sector hubs and then verified commercial options. Provider pages link to SER-03 and issuer evidence. Candidate flow is SER-04 → SER-05 → SER-06 → SER-09/SER-10/SER-12. Employer flow is SER-08 → SER-03/SER-11 → SER-09/SER-10/SER-12.

## Evidence and editorial standards

- Use current primary regulator, accreditation/licensing, scheme-owner, LSP/issuer, and legal sources; record access and verification dates.
- Never imply the site is BNSP, KADIN, an LSP, regulator, accreditor, licensing body, assessor, or issuer without verified legal authority.
- Never promise passing, certification, recognition, employment, salary, promotion, compliance, or license eligibility.
- Clearly name the actual provider, assessor, issuer, TUK, counterparty, privacy controller, complaint body, and refund owner.
- Legal, accreditation, K3, regulated-occupation, privacy, accessibility, and international-recognition content requires qualified review.
